# Data Function Introduction:
**++Shop vs Account level data++**
For all Partners that are **++not++** under the Shop Management category, you will notice differences in GMV data between Partner Centre and Seller Centre. This is because, as a TikTok Shop Partner (TSP), when you bind to a seller, the GMV data displayed to you in the Partner Centre is only at **++Account++** level. However, the GMV data displayed on Seller Center is at **++Shop++** level.
**++What are the differences in calculation between Account level and Shop level data?++**
A seller can have up to 5 accounts: 1 Official Account + 4 Marketing Account
Therefore Shop level data includes GMV from **++all the accounts++** associated with the seller, including the Shop Page. However, as a TSP, you might only bind to the sellers Marketing Account, which means in the Partner Centre you only see Marketing Account level data.
++For example (see image below):++ You are a TSP operating under the Content Management service category and are interested in Seller A who has 1 Official Account and 2 Marketing Accounts. However, you only bind to the 2 Marketing Accounts of Seller A.
Therefore, you will see differences in GMV data between Seller A's Seller Centre data (where total GMV will include GMV from the 1 Official Account and 2 Marketing Accounts) and the GMV data you see on Partner Centre (which only includes GMV from 2 Marketing Accounts).
# **Data Overview**
How to use Data Overview?

* To understand the overall transaction performance and sales rate of the seller related accounts, and to see the percentage of sellers with sales, to determine the activity of its sellers and their ability to sell goods.
* Analyze the live/video content channels to analyze the ability of the seller to sell goods, the number of content releases and the CTR conversion rate.

**++Content Type GMV Attribution++**
GMV for sellers will be calculated based on 3 content types, which means now: ****

* **100% GMV (Direct GMV) = Live + Video + Product Card**

This means that for sellers, GMV will be attributed to the last content type that a buyer clicked on when checking out a product. *(Reminder: Product Cards are cards that display everything a customer needs to know about a product, like its title, image, and price).*
All Seller Transaction related metrics such as GMV, C_O, GPM on every module are calculated using this Content Type GMV Attribution.
++For example:++ Buyer A watched a LIVE session promoting an iPhone 15 and adds the iPhone 15 to cart > Buyer A checks out the iPhone 15 from the cart 2 days later > GMV from the iPhone 15 goes to **LIVE**.
*Please note, there will be no changes to how Affiliate GMV is calculated, which means that there will be no changes to how commission paid to Affiliates.*
## Core Data

* **Livestreaming Partners:** The number of partners that have livestreamed during the selected period
* **Active Rate:** (Active Live Partners/ Partners) * 100%
* **Partners with Sales:** The number of partners that generated in revenue during the selected period
* **Sales Rate:** (Partners with Sales/ Partners) * 100%

## GMV trend

* **Direct GMV:** This is the total amount paid for orders that are placed directly from LIVEs + Videos + Product Card, including returns and refunds.
* **LIVE Direct GMV:** LIVE direct GMV is the total amount paid for orders that are placed directly from LIVEs, including returns and refunds. This means that GMV is calculated based on the last clicked livestream and excludes any form of attributed GMV
* **LIVE Avg. Direct GMV**: LIVE Direct GMV/ LIVE stream
* **Direct Refund GMV:** The cumulative amount refunded to buyers during the selected period based on Direct GMV achieved. This means that the Refund GMV only calculates GMV from refunded orders based on buyer's last clicked content type and excludes any form of refunded orders from attributed GMV.
* **Video Direct GMV:** Video direct GMV is the total amount paid for orders placed directly from videos, including returns and refunds. This means that GMV is only calculated based on the last clicked video and excludes any form of attributed GMV

## Livestream

* **LIVE Orders:** The number of orders created and paid by users from partners LIVE streams
* **LIVE Product Sales:** The cumulative number of products sold from Live streams by partners during the selected period
* **Livestreams:** The number of in-house livestreams/livestreams by partners during the selected period
* **LIVE Duration:** The cumulative duration of in-house livestreams/livestreams by partners during the selected period
* **LIVE Avg. CTR:** (Number of product clicks / number of livestream views) / livestreams

## EC video trend

* **Video Orders:** The number of SKU orders created and paid by users from partner videos
* **Video Product Sales:** The cumulative number of products sold from partner videos during the selected period
* **Videos**: The number of videos posted by partners during the selected period
* **Avg. CTR:** (Number of product clicks / number of video views) / number of videos

## Q&A

1. Why is it impossible to see video details for some video?
   * Videos, whose status are set to private or deleted, do not support playback and cannot display video pictures;
   * Videos, whose status are set to private and have never been made public, have no transaction or content data;
2. Why are the recommended videos with high revenue not shown in the transaction analysis in the video detail page?
   * For videos with multiple product links, you need to select one product in the video detail page-transaction analysis, and the video of the TOP3 revenue amount in all videos (including the sellers own short video and creators short videos) with the same product will be displayed below the transaction analysis.
